Habitat for Humanity

I have been involved in Habitat for Humanity since high school. This organization helps members of the community achieve their dream of homeownership by using volunteer labor to build affordable housing. I have relished the opportunity to engage with and serve my community in this way while also learning exciting new skills in construction. I am grateful to have had the opportunity to orchestrate and lead a group of my peers in a Habitat build through my position on the community service branch of MIT’s engineering honor society, Tau Beta Pi. While I had hoped to organize more events this year to introduce my peers to the incredibly gratifying experience of contributing to a Habitat build, I am excited to do so whenever such activities can resume safely.



Greater Boston Food Bank

I was first introduced to the GBFB through a Tau Beta Pi community service event in my sophomore year. I was thrilled to see the impact of our small group’s efforts and was eager to contribute further. It occured to me that the structure of MIT dining and cook-for-yourself systems produced a great deal of food waste - especially at the end of the year when many students and staff relocate for the summer term. Seeing this excess inspired me to organize a food drive, benefitting the GBFB, to help alleviate food insecurity for our neighbors in the Boston area community. As many prepared to leave campus for the summer, I gathered a few friends to help distribute collection boxes and donation guidelines. The response was stunning; donation boxes overflowed and we were barely able to fit them all into our van. The food drive has become an annual event, and even this past year I was able to throw together a last minute drive to help sustain the food bank amid the pandemic evacuations and collected a surprising volume of donations (pictured) despite the haste.
